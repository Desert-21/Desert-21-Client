How to play?

In a nutshell:

  • This is a board game, and the board consists of fields
  • Field can be owned by a player, or unoccupied. It can also contain a building, giving owner a strategic advantage.
  • Your goal is to conquer enemy field with Home Base, before you opponent conquers yours
  • You do this by performing actions in each turn. You upgrade existing buildings, build new ones and train army by clicking things on the right panel. You do the lab by clicking the blue button on the top. You attack, move units, bombard or fire rockets by dragging and dropping the cursor over the fields on the board. You submit these actions with the green button on the top.

Detailed guide

Goal of the game

As a player, you are thrown into a desert in far future, when machines fight for humans. You are thrown there together with your enemy though. Your goal is to by better commanding you army conquer enemy Home Base, before they conquer yours!

Game stages

The game is based on turns, switching between you and your opponent. Before it begins you will both get some time to look around then, it goes like this:

  • You make your move by performing some actions and confirming them by clicking the big green button on the top. These actions are executed all together, after the end of turn and their order doesn't matter. One move usually consists of many actions - around 5 at the beginning, increasing later in later stages of the game.
  • The resolution phase begins. It's a short stage between moves, when you can see the results of your actions, whether is some training finished or a battle won.
  • Your opponent makes their moves, just as you did a moment before. At this time you can still pre-move some actions, which will speed up your next turn or at least give you some more time to think. Be careful though! If you opponent makes them impossible to perform, some of them might be cancelled.
  • The resolution phase for your opponent begins. It looks just like your own resolution phase, but you will notice, that some things are kept in secret from you.
  • You make your turn again. The circle repeats until somebody looses...

Game elements

  • Fields are what the board consists of. They can be owned by a player, or unoccupied. Fields can be conquered by players, either by defeating opponents army or scarabs if not owned by any player. Fields can be empty, or contain a single building, giving owner specific perks or allowing for specific actions.
  • Actions are the things you can do during your turn. They are executed all together after the turn finishes and are the way you interact with the game.
  • Army is the group of battle units spread across the fields. They are automatically defending owners fields against enemy attacks, but can be also used to attack (and conquer, if succeeded) a neighbouring field.
  • Resources (metal, building materials and electricity) are the materials needed to execute some actions, like producting your battle units or upgrading buildings. You gain resources in your Home Base, factories or thanks to lab upgrades.
  • Lab upgrades are the global and permanent bonuses you unlock using electricity. They are grouped into three branches (Combat, Control and Production). Each branch works like a tree of hierarchy, where unlocking higher tier upgrades needs upgrading at least one from the lower tier.
  • Buildings are what can be found on some fields, that gives owners certain benefits, like better resource production. Almost all of them can be upgraded and after doing some lab, they can be also built on empty fields.

Game view

  • Game header is the top part of the screen when you can find an "End Turn" button with timer, your current available resources, the lab, some basic info about the game and options panel, where you can for example surrender.
  • Game board is the bunch of squares in the middle. Here you can see what is happening in the game, but also perform some army movements actions by a drag-and-drop technique from field to field. You can also select a field by clicking on it to view it's details on the right. The board is enlighting the fields with greater intensity based on the strength of the army defending it.
  • Field details is on the right panel, where you can see some details of the field you selected on the board. Here you can see what exactly defends it, what building it holds (if any), and you can perform training, building and upgrading actions there.
  • Current actions are on the left panel, where you can see a list of current actions that will be executed after the end of your turn. Here you may view their cost, some details about them, but also cancel them if needed. Some actions will show the affected fields on the board too when you hover on them.

Performing actions

  • Training units is performed on the bottom of the right panel, on the field selection. It can be only performed on a field which contains a Tower or a Home Base. You can train three different types of units (droids, tanks and cannons) in three different production modes (small, medium or mass production). Higher production modes last the same amount of time as the lower ones, but cost more and produce more units during the time period.
  • Upgrading building is performed on the top of the right panel, on the field selection. It increases bonuses the building gives to the owner, also when opponent conquers the field from you. You can upgrade all buildings except Rocket Launchers.
  • Lab upgrade is performed by clicking on the blue button on header. Upgrades are grouped into three branches (Combat, Control and Production). Each branch works like a tree of hierarchy, where unlocking higher tier upgrades needs upgrading at least one from the lower tier.
  • Moving units is performed by dragging and dropping mouse from field to field on the board. It can be performed only on your fields and path of your units cannot cross opponents fields. Different units have different marching speeds, that limit the amount of fields that can be traveled through in a single turn. The path indicator that appearing on drag and drop showing the path you units will take. When hovering on more distanced fields, it will not grow any longer than the maximum length of path that the fastest unit can travel.
  • Attacking is performed just like moving units, but on dropping above an opponents field instead of yours. The rules of how long can units travel remain the same. You can attack a single field from many different fields, which will make attacks combine into powerful one. If you win the battle, the attacked field becomes yours and combined units (weakened by the defenders) will be defending the newly conquered field. If you loose - you loose all your units.
  • Bombarding is a type of attacking that can be performed only by cannons, after improving them in the lab. It is weaker than regular attack, but your cannons are untouched by the defenders and remain on the same field they attacked from. Bombarding can be a good way to poke your opponent and giving them some free damage.
  • Firing rockets is an action performed by drag-and-drop from field to field, but only from a field with a rocket launcher. It will destroy 50% of army on the targeted field, no matter how big it is. It is the best way to punish opponent for keeping too many units on a single field or weakening a field before big attack.
  • Building new building is performed on the top of the right panel, on the field selection. It can be only performed on empty fields and after upgrading specific lab upgrades. It lasts 2 turns and makes the field not empty anymore, but containing a selected level 1 building.
